Project Overview

A River That Has Never Been Used Like This

The Kolpa is the longest Slovenian river coast — 113 kilometres of borderland shared with Croatia, protected under Natura 2000 and recognised as one of Europe's most intact river ecosystems. It is already Slovenia's warmest river, a canoe corridor, a fishing haven. What it has never been is a platform for learning, wellness, and cultural exchange that floats directly upon it.

This project proposes three modular, solar-powered floating platforms — anchored seasonally on different stretches of the Kolpa river between Metlika, Semič and Črnomelj — each dedicated to a distinct programme stream: wellness and yoga, youth eco-learning and science camps, and cultural storytelling rooted in Bela Krajina border folklore. The platforms are reached by e-bike trail connections, making the whole system car-free by design.

Each platform is modular: manufactured off-site in sections, transported by road, assembled on water with minimal disruption to the riverbed. Solar panels provide the energy for lighting, audio equipment and climate-resilient sheltering. In summer the platforms host outdoor programming; a winter wellness configuration uses insulated modular shelters for retreat and contemplation. The river becomes the setting, not the obstacle.

Cross-border in its DNA, the project builds on the precedent set by the EU-funded Misterion project (Semič, Metlika, Kamanje, HR — ERDF, 2014-2020, EUR 1.24M) and extends its cultural logic into the water itself. Croatian artists, cultural associations and wellness practitioners become resident contributors. Bela Krajina's unique position as a Slovenian-Croatian borderland becomes its competitive advantage.

Why Floating?

A floating structure leaves zero permanent footprint on the riverbank. No excavation, no piling, no land-use change. The platform moves with seasonal water levels and can be removed entirely in winter. For a Natura 2000-protected river, this distinction is critical — and it is the strongest argument in the permit conversation with regulators.

Regulatory Considerations

This is the most complex section of the project — and the reason it must start with a regulatory consultation, not a funding application. Floating structures on Slovenian rivers require permits under the Zakon o vodah (ZV-1) (Water Act), coordinated through ARSO (Slovenian Environment Agency) and implemented by the relevant VGP (Vodnogospodarski zavod — Water Management Institute) regional office. None of this is an insurmountable obstacle, but it cannot be bypassed or left for later.

Vodno Soglasje (Water Consent)

Any structure placed in or on a Slovenian river requires a vodno soglasje (water permit / consent) under Article 153 ZV-1. This is issued by ARSO after assessment of hydrological impact. For floating, anchored structures, the assessment focuses on water flow interference, flood risk, and ecological impact. Modular, seasonally removable platforms are viewed more favourably than permanent structures.

Natura 2000 Compliance

The Kolpa is Natura 2000 protected (EU Habitats Directive). An appropriate assessment (presoja ustreznosti) is required if the project could affect the conservation status of protected habitats or species. Key concern: disturbance to nesting birds and fish habitat during construction. Seasonal deployment (April–October) and removable anchoring significantly reduce this risk. Environmental consultant needed for this assessment.

Spatial Planning (OPN)

Each municipality has an OPN (Občinski prostorski načrt) governing land and water use. River activities may fall under special-use zone provisions. Coordination with Metlika, Semič and Črnomelj municipal planning offices is needed to confirm that floating seasonal structures do not require a formal spatial planning amendment (OPPN). In most cases temporary installations do not.

Navigation & Safety

The Kolpa is used by canoeists and small pleasure craft. Platforms must be marked with navigation buoys and must not obstruct the navigable channel. Coordination with the Inland Waterways Authority (Uprava RS za pomorstvo) may be needed depending on the specific moorings. Platform design must include safety railings and emergency egress per Slovenian occupational safety standards.

Border Zone Sensitivity

Parts of the Kolpa lie directly on the SI-HR state border. Structures near or on the border require coordination with the Police Directorate Novo Mesto (border affairs) and potentially MZZ (Foreign Ministry) for cross-border activities hosted on the platform. In practice this is procedural, not prohibitive — the Misterion project navigated similar issues.

Construction & Transport

Platform sections are built off-site (in a workshop/boatyard) and transported to the river by road, then assembled on water. This avoids riverbank construction disturbance. Access roads to launching points must be confirmed. Weight limits on rural bridges in the area need checking — some cross-Kolpa bridges are lightly rated.

The Cross-Border Opportunity

The Kolpa river is not just an asset — it is a shared asset, belonging equally to Slovenia and Croatia. The EU has already funded cross-border collaboration on this exact stretch through the Misterion project (ERDF, €1.24M, 2014-2020), which created the Bela Krajina Nature Centre in Semič and archaeological heritage work on the Croatian side. That precedent makes the Kolpa Floating Hubs a natural next chapter — building on established relationships, moving from cultural heritage into living culture and contemporary wellness.

  • Artist residencies with Croatian cultural organisations — 3–5 residencies per year, 2 weeks each
  • Joint SI-HR folklore and music evenings on Platform 03 — bilingual programming
  • Cross-border youth eco-camp exchange — Slovenian and Croatian school groups together
  • Co-promoted wellness tourism package — marketed jointly to Ljubljana, Zagreb and Vienna visitors
  • Shared monitoring of Kolpa river ecology — citizen science across both banks
  • Pathway to Interreg SI-HR application in Phase 2 (2029–2030) — scale the model

Interreg SI-HR Precedent: Misterion project — Total investment €1,239,205 — ERDF contribution €1,053,325 — Municipalities Semič, Metlika (SI) + Kamanje (HR). Source: EC Regional Policy, Inforegio.

Honest Risk Assessment

Risks & Mitigations

High Risk

Regulatory / Permit Delays

Water consent and Natura 2000 assessment could take 12+ months and potentially be refused or require major redesign of platform size, location, or anchoring method.

Mitigation: Start regulatory process before submitting LAS application. Engage VGP informally first. Build 6-month buffer into timeline. Commission preliminary Natura 2000 screening early.
High Risk

Budget Ceiling Stretch

€250,000 is at the top of what LAS DBK typically funds in a single project. The ESRR envelope (€1.817M total) means this project alone consumes 14% of all available ESRR funds — raising competitiveness concerns.

Mitigation: Consider phasing — Platform 01 only in first application (€95K), with platforms 02-03 in subsequent call. Or combine with Erasmus+ for youth camp component to reduce LAS ask.
Medium Risk

Seasonal Operational Viability

High water events, floods, and winter conditions on the Kolpa could make the platforms unusable for extended periods, reducing utilisation and programming revenue.

Mitigation: Platform design must include rapid-release mooring for flood events. Seasonal removal protocol built into operational plan. Insurance for weather disruption included in budget.
Medium Risk

Programming Sustainability

After the grant period, maintaining year-round programming quality requires dedicated staff and ongoing revenue. Rural areas can struggle to sustain premium wellness/cultural programming.

Mitigation: Revenue model includes commercial wellness operators using platforms on a concession basis. Community ownership model (cooperative/zadružna oblika) builds local commitment to sustainability.
Low Risk

Cross-Border Complexity

Croatian partner engagement requires MoU formalities and cross-border coordination. Staff and participants crossing the border adds administrative overhead.

Mitigation: Misterion established exactly these partnerships — use existing relationships. SI-HR is an EU internal border, Schengen since 2023. Border zone coordination is procedural, not prohibitive.
Low Risk

Technology & Maintenance

Floating solar platforms and modular pontoon systems are mature technologies with falling costs. Off-grid solar for small-scale loads is well-understood.

Mitigation: Use CE-certified European platform manufacturers with established maintenance contracts. Solar module costs at historic lows (~€0.13/Wp, 2025). Include 3-year maintenance contract in budget.
What To Do Now

Next Steps — Practical Preparation

  1. Call VGP Sava — Book the Preliminary Meeting

    This is the single most important first step. Request an informal consultancy meeting to understand what floating structures require on the Kolpa. Get their read before investing in design or permits. Contact: VGP Sava d.o.o., Novo Mesto office.

  2. Contact LAS DBK Office Directly

    Call or email LAS DBK in Novo Mesto to ask: (a) when is the next ESRR open call, (b) is floating infrastructure eligible as "minor infrastructure investment", (c) can a phased project be submitted across two calls. Website: las-dbk.si

  3. Commission a Natura 2000 Pre-Screening

    Hire an environmental consultant for a preliminary Natura 2000 screening report. This document will be essential for the water consent application and reassures the LAS DBK selection committee that environmental risks are understood.

  4. Identify Lead Applicant Entity

    Decide whether an NGO, a municipality, or a new entity leads. The municipality carries more credibility for infrastructure co-financing but may be slower. A local zavod (public institute) or novo društvo (new association) with municipal backing is often the sweet spot.

  5. Reach Out to Croatian Cultural Organisations

    Contact cultural associations in the Karlovac county and Kupa valley area to gauge interest in a residency partnership. A signed letter of intent from a Croatian partner significantly strengthens cross-border narrative in the LAS application.

  6. Request Preliminary Platform Design Sketch

    Get a concept-level sketch from an architect or marine/civil engineer to confirm what 3 modular platforms at ~30m² each look like, cost, and what mooring footprint they require. This is not a full design — just enough to validate the budget and present to regulators and LAS DBK.

  7. Explore Erasmus+ KA2 for the Youth Camp Component

    The cross-border youth eco-camp (SI + HR school groups) is a near-perfect fit for Erasmus+ KA2 (Strategic Partnerships). A parallel Erasmus+ application for the youth component reduces the LAS DBK ask and adds a second funding source.
