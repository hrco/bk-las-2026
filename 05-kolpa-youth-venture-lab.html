Project Overview

Growing the Next Generation of Green Entrepreneurs — Right Here

Rural youth drain is one of the most stubborn challenges facing Slovenia's southeastern regions. Young people from Bela Krajina leave not because the land offers nothing — the Kolpa river, the birch forests, the untouched valleys and centuries of herb and food culture are an extraordinary inheritance — but because nobody has shown them how to build a livelihood from it. The Kolpa Youth Green Venture Lab changes that calculus directly.

Over six months, 50 participants aged 15–26 from schools and youth communities in Semič, Črnomelj, and Metlika will move from idea to prototype to pitch. The curriculum is built around the Kolpa river corridor and Bela Krajina's natural bounty: locally harvested wild herbs (elder flower, chamomile, calendula, yarrow, linden), river clay, beeswax, walnut oil — all proven raw material for bio-cosmetics, food supplements, and wellness products already gaining traction in Slovenian export markets. Alongside green product development, participants will explore river tourism — guided kayak experiences, eco-camping and rafting — and local food brands rooted in protected regional identity.

Mentorship is local. Farmers, tourism operators, existing small producers, and guest entrepreneurs from across the region will coach participants week-by-week. The program culminates in a cross-border pitch fair with Croatian youth organisations along the Kolpa, turning the natural border into a collaboration bridge. With an EU co-financing rate of up to 80% under ESRR through LAS DBK, the net cost to any local applicant is minimal — while the community benefit compounds for years.

Context: LAS DBK (LAS Dolenjska in Bela Krajina) has a confirmed budget of €4.073M for 2021–2027, of which €1.817M is ESRR allocated for entrepreneurship, quality of life, and socially inclusive projects. Youth entrepreneurship academies have been explicitly cited as a priority activity type in Slovenia's LEADER framework — and score highly against EU evaluation criteria.

Program Design

The 6-Month Academy Structure

Three phases, each two months long, taking participants from raw inspiration to a market-ready pitch. Meetings twice weekly, half in school settings, half in the field.

Phase 01
Explore & Ideate
Months 1–2 — Foundation
  • Field trips: Kolpa river, local farms, herb gardens, beekeepers
  • Mapping Bela Krajina's natural & cultural assets
  • Entrepreneurship basics: customer discovery, market sizing
  • Workshops with local producers and tourism operators
  • Team formation — participants self-select into venture groups
  • First pitch: "What problem do we solve, for whom?"
Phase 02
Build & Prototype
Months 3–4 — Creation
  • Product/service prototyping with allocated micro-budget
  • Bio-cosmetics lab sessions: herb extraction, formulation basics
  • Eco-tourism route design with local guides
  • Brand identity: naming, packaging, simple website/social
  • First real customer test — sell to 10 people
  • Mentorship check-ins: weekly 1-on-1 with mentor
Phase 03
Refine & Pitch
Months 5–6 — Launch
  • Business model refinement and financial projections
  • Legal basics: sole trader registration, food/cosmetics regs
  • Press kit and pitch deck preparation
  • Regional pitch rehearsal — local jury feedback
  • Cross-border Pitch Fair (SI–HR): public event on Kolpa
  • Post-program: 3-month light follow-up & network access

How to Apply

Requirements & Eligibility at a Glance

Who Can Be Lead Applicant
  • Secondary school in the LAS DBK area (e.g. SŠ Črnomelj)
  • Registered NGO / youth organisation in Semič, Črnomelj or Metlika
  • Municipal youth office or public institute (zavod)
  • Zavod za razvoj in kulturo Bele krajine or equivalent regional body
  • Consortium of the above with clear lead partner
Key Funding Rules (ESRR via LAS DBK)
  • EU co-financing: up to 80% of eligible costs
  • Applicant must provide 20% own contribution (cash or in-kind)
  • Project must be implemented in LAS DBK territory
  • Project must align with at least one LAS DBK strategy priority axis
  • Participants must be from LAS DBK municipalities
Eligible Costs
  • Facilitator and trainer fees (internal or external)
  • Mentor honoraria and travel costs
  • Materials for prototyping (cosmetic ingredients, packaging, tools)
  • Event organisation costs (pitch fair venue, catering, print)
  • Communication and visibility (project website, printed materials)
  • Project management and documentation (up to 15% of budget)
Documents Needed to Apply
  • Completed LAS DBK application form (prijavnica)
  • Detailed project description and work plan
  • Itemised budget with justification
  • Partnership letters of intent (from all partners)
  • Proof of legal status of lead applicant
  • De minimis declaration (if applicable)

Cross-Border Youth Pitch Fair

The culminating event of the academy will be held on or near the Kolpa — either at an outdoor venue in Bela Krajina or at a partner site in the Croatian Kolpa area (Karlovac, Ozalj or Vivodina). Both Slovenian and Croatian youth ventures will present. A mixed jury of local entrepreneurs, tourism professionals and LAS representatives awards recognition prizes. Local media from both countries are invited.

This dimension costs very little to add (it is absorbed into the Pitch Fair budget line) but substantially increases the application's European character — a factor LEADER evaluators reward.

Getting Started

Next Steps — From Idea to Application

1

Contact LAS DBK & Confirm the Open Call

Visit las-dbk.si and check whether an ESRR call is currently open or expected. Contact the LAS DBK office in Novo Mesto to get a briefing on current priorities and any upcoming public calls for the 2021–2027 period. Ask specifically about youth and green economy measures under ESRR.

2

Identify the Lead Applicant

Decide whether this will be led by the school (SŠ Črnomelj), a local NGO/zavod, or the municipality. The lead applicant must be a legal entity in the LAS DBK territory with capacity to manage EU funds (i.e., can open a separate bank account for the project, has accounting in order).

3

Assemble Partner Letters of Intent

Reach out informally to 3–4 key partners: a school, a local entrepreneur association, one or two farmer/beekeeper mentors, and a Croatian youth organisation if available. Informal conversations first — formal letters come after the lead applicant is decided.

4

Draft the Project Description

Use the structure in this document as a starting point. The application form (prijavnica) will ask for: project rationale, target group, activities, timeline, expected outputs, and budget breakdown. Allow 3–4 weeks for a strong draft.

5

Secure the 20% Own Contribution

€8,000. Options: municipal co-financing contribution, school in-kind contribution (space, staff time), NGO membership fees or existing grants, or a combination. In-kind contributions (volunteer hours, premises) are often eligible — confirm with LAS DBK.

6

Submit & Follow Up

Submit before the call deadline. After submission, stay in regular contact with the LAS DBK contact person — they can flag any formal issues early. Evaluation typically takes 6–10 weeks. If approved, the project starts within 3 months of the decision.
