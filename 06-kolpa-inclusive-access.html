Project Overview

Everyone Deserves the Kolpa

The Kolpa River is one of the warmest rivers in Slovenia — a turquoise ribbon threading through Bela Krajina's karst meadows, oak forests, and border villages. Tens of thousands of visitors come each summer. But for people with mobility impairments, wheelchair users, elderly visitors, or families with young children, the riverbanks remain largely out of reach: steep, uneven paths, no adapted launch points, no equipment.

This project creates five barrier-free river access nodes along the Kolpa — from Vinica to Metlika — each with a gently sloped wheelchair ramp directly to the water, adapted kayak equipment with pontoon-assisted boarding, EV charging for electric mobility aids, and smart biodiversity interpretation panels. Every node connects to the existing Čredniška pot / Poljanska dolina trail network that LAS DBK has already supported, extending the accessible tourism ecosystem in a coherent, additive way.

A digital layer transforms each node into an augmented reality heritage stop. Visitors scan a QR marker with their smartphones — no app install required — and see historical overlays: border life under Yugoslavia, folklore of the Kolpa water spirits, abandoned water mills, the stories of families who lived by this river for generations. Accessible adventure. Accessible culture. Year-round.

Digital Heritage Layer

Augmented Reality: The River's Memory

At each node, a weatherproof QR marker post (EU-standard signage design) activates a web-based AR experience — no app download required. Visitors point their smartphone camera and see the Kolpa's past layered over its present.

Node 01 — Vinica: Border Life Under Yugoslavia

Overlay of the border guard routes, smuggler paths, and the daily life of river communities split by a political border. Photographs from the 1950s–80s float over the real landscape.

Node 02 — Adlešiči: The Water Mills

A 3D reconstruction of the Kolpa's historic mlin (water mill) network appears on ruins still visible at the riverbank. Visitors can "walk through" the mill as it was in 1900.

Node 03 — Dragatuš: Partisans & Crossings

WWII resistance movement routes overlaid on the landscape. Secret crossing points, safe houses, the geography of clandestine wartime life made visible and speakable.

Node 04 — Stari Trg: Livestock Fairs & Trade

Historical scenes of the famous livestock fairs — merchant routes, animals, the social life of Bela Krajina's agricultural economy — appear on the village square and trail start.

Node 05 — Metlika: Wine, Water & River Culture

Metlika's winemaking heritage, the Kolpa's role in trade, and folk stories of the river water spirits (povodni mož) come alive through illustrated overlays and audio narration.

How the AR System Works

1

Scan the QR Marker Post

Weatherproof steel post at each node. Standard QR code — any smartphone camera works. No app, no download, no account.

2

Web AR Loads in Browser

Built on open standards (WebXR + AR.js or 8th Wall WebAR). GPS + compass positioning overlays content on the real camera view. Works on iOS Safari and Android Chrome.

3

Accessible Content Modes

Each story available in: visual AR overlay, text-only mode (screen reader compatible), audio narration (headphone friendly), and simplified easy-read version.

4

Languages

Slovenian, Croatian, English. Optional German (tourism market). Content hosted on municipal server — no third-party dependency for long-term sustainability.

5

Content Partners

Historical photographs and archive material from Belokranjski muzej Metlika. WWII material from Muzej novejše zgodovine. Local oral history collected via Karlovac University partnership.

EU Precedent: The Aure4rural Interreg project (France/Spain) and the Cyprus Deputy Ministry of Tourism AR heritage app both demonstrate EU-funded QR-based rural heritage AR. This approach is proven, fundable, and scalable. Europeana Pro explicitly endorses XR in rural cultural heritage as a priority use case for 2021–2027.

Cross-Border Opportunity

The Croatian Connection

🇦🇴 ↔ 🇭🇷

An Accessible Border Route Along the Kolpa

The Kolpa forms the international border between Slovenia and Croatia for much of its length. Croatia's Karlovac County has active rural development programs and accessible tourism initiatives. A coordinated cross-border accessible water trail — where ramps on both banks are designed to the same standard — would be a first-of-its-kind feature in the region.

Even without formal cross-border project structure, design coordination with Croatian partners qualifies as a "cooperation" element in LAS project scoring.

Cooperation Points to Develop

  • Joint design standard for ramp slope and width — matching SR EN ISO 10865 accessible water access guidance
  • Shared AR content layer covering Croatian-side stories (border villages, shared folklore)
  • Coordinated accessible kayak route: launch in Slovenia, land in Croatia, return by road — legal cross-border recreational passage
  • Potential future Interreg SI-HR 2021–2027 top-up funding for cross-border infrastructure phase
  • Partner contact: Javna ustanova za upravljanje zaštićenim područjima Karlovačke županije (Croatia)
  • Mutual recognition of accessible tourism certification (both countries aligning with European Accessibility Act by June 2025)
Getting Started

Practical Next Steps

This is a brainstorm document — not yet a formal application. These steps turn the concept into a submission-ready project.

Check the Current Call

Visit las-dbk.si/javni-pozivi and confirm whether a new EKSRP public call is open or announced. Ask LAS DBK directly for the expected call timeline if none is posted.

Identify the Lead Partner

Decide between Občina Metlika, Občina Črnomelj, or CUDV Črnomelj as lead applicant. The choice affects which budget lines are eligible and the co-financing rate. Call a short meeting with all three.

Site Walk with CUDV

Do a joint site survey along the Kolpa with CUDV Črnomelj. Bring a wheelchair user. Real accessibility assessment of candidate locations will strengthen the application narrative enormously.

Secure 3 Cost Estimates

Get preliminary quotes from civil engineering firms for ramp construction, an adaptive sports equipment supplier (check Interreg Europe good practices list), and a WebXR development studio.

Contact Belokranjski Muzej

Meet with the museum director about archive licensing for the AR layer. This costs little but massively strengthens the cultural heritage scoring. They may also co-write the application.

Map the Land

Confirm all five candidate node locations are on public land (GURS cadastral map) or that land use agreements can be arranged. Water law permits via ARSO will be needed for any structure in the river or within 5m of the bank.

Pre-Application Meeting

Book a pre-application consultation with LAS DBK. They are legally required to advise applicants and will confirm eligibility, budget ceiling, and scoring criteria before you invest time in the full application.

Write the Application

Use the LAS DBK application template (from the call documentation). This concept document is your starting point for the project description section. Budget for 20–40 hours of writing and document collection time.
